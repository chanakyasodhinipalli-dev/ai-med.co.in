Biology Is Entering a New Era

For centuries, biological systems were studied through observation and experimentation. Today, advances in artificial intelligence, computational biology, and multi-omics technologies are transforming how life itself is understood.

At the forefront of this transformation is Dr. Pawan K. Dhar — a molecular biologist and synthetic biology pioneer whose work bridges biology, computation, and policy. As Chief Executive Officer of AI.MeD, he leads the company’s mission to build AI-native platforms that transform biological complexity into structured, scalable intelligence.

The future of life sciences will be driven by intelligent platforms capable of decoding, modeling, and engineering biological systems.

Scientific Background & Leadership

Over four decades of interdisciplinary research spanning biology, computation, and governance

Dr. Dhar is a Professor of Synthetic Biology at the School of Biotechnology, Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), New Delhi, and former Dean of the School of Biotechnology. His research spans molecular biology, systems biology, genomics, computational biology, synthetic biology, and biotechnology policy — with over 75 peer-reviewed scientific publications cited by 2,000+ researchers worldwide.

Academic Leadership

Professor & former Dean at JNU, New Delhi. Previously held senior positions at RIKEN Genomic Sciences Centre (Japan), Keio University (Japan), Bioinformatics Institute (Singapore), Shiv Nadar University, and University of Kerala.

Pioneering Research

Pioneered the demonstration that naturally non-expressing intergenic DNA sequences can be synthetically expressed to produce functional molecules. Explored the “dark genome” — non-expressing, non-translating, and extinct DNA sequences as untapped genomic resources.

Scientific Publishing

75+ peer-reviewed publications. Founding Editor-in-Chief of Springer’s Systems and Synthetic Biology journal. Co-editor of the textbook Systems and Synthetic Biology (Springer) and E-Cell System: Basic Concepts and Applications.

Global Impact

Represented India at key global synthetic biology meetings. Active contributor to national and international dialogues on biotechnology governance, biosafety, and ethical innovation. Speaker at BioAsia and international conferences.

Vision for AI-Native Life Sciences Platforms

The life sciences ecosystem today generates unprecedented volumes of biological data. However, the challenge lies not in generating data — but in transforming that data into meaningful biological intelligence.

Dr. Dhar’s scientific philosophy views living systems as information-driven networks — complex biological systems that can be interpreted, modeled, and responsibly engineered using advanced computational approaches. This perspective forms the intellectual foundation of AI.MeD’s technology platform.

Leadership Philosophy

Three core principles guiding the company’s approach to building transformative biological intelligence systems

Scientific Rigor

Innovation must always be grounded in credible science and reproducible research. Every platform, pipeline, and model must meet the highest standards of scientific integrity.

Responsible Innovation

Advances in biotechnology must be guided by ethical governance, biosafety standards, and long-term societal responsibility — not just commercial opportunity.

Platform Thinking

Scalable scientific progress requires integrated platforms rather than isolated technologies. The operating system approach unlocks compounding value across the ecosystem.

Building the Future of Intelligent Biology

Artificial intelligence is reshaping every scientific discipline — and biology is no exception. The ability to combine computational intelligence with molecular science will define the next generation of breakthroughs in medicine, agriculture, environmental science, and biotechnology.

Dr. Dhar believes that this transformation requires not only new technologies, but new ways of thinking about biological systems themselves.

Under Dr. Dhar’s stewardship, AI.MeD is not building another pipeline manager. It is building the next-generation operating platform for modern biology — designed to empower researchers, accelerate discovery, and enable scalable innovation across the global life sciences ecosystem.
